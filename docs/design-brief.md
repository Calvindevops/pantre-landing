# Pantrẽ Landing Page — Design Brief

## Purpose
Pantrẽ solves "what should I cook tonight?" for home cooks who have ingredients but lack inspiration and confidence. The landing page converts curious visitors into beta waitlist signups by making them FEEL what cooking with AI would be like — not just telling them.

## Tone: Editorial Luxury meets Organic Warmth
This is Bon Appetit's design language meets Linear's product polish. Premium but not cold. Confident but not corporate. The italic Instrument Serif whispers sophistication; the clean UI cards say "this actually works." Dark backgrounds for immersion (the cooking assistant lives here), warm whites for clarity (the fridge, the recipes). Green is never decorative — it means life, freshness, GO.

## Constraints
- Next.js 16 App Router, Tailwind 4, TypeScript strict
- LCP < 2.5s — generative canvas must not block first paint (lazy load, dynamic import)
- Mobile-first — 70%+ traffic will be mobile
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — canvas degrades to static gradient
- Fonts: Instrument Serif (display), Plus Jakarta Sans (body) — already loaded
- WCAG AA contrast on all text

## Differentiation: The Living Kitchen
The ONE unforgettable thing: a generative particle system that looks and feels like heat rising from a stovetop — subtle green convection currents flowing behind the app mockups. No other cooking app has algorithmic art on their landing page. Visitors will remember "that cooking app with the living background." The algorithm IS the brand — computation expressing the physics of cooking.

## Design Brief (North Star)
Pantrẽ's landing page is an editorial food magazine that's alive. Every element earns its space — the italic serif headlines set the tone of someone who takes cooking seriously but not preciously, the full-bleed mockup showcase proves the product is real, and the generative convection canvas beneath it all creates an ambient warmth that makes visitors feel like they're already in the kitchen. The page converts through FEELING, not features. Black buttons, never green (green means organic life, not "click here"). One waitlist form, no friction. The experience should feel like opening a beautiful cookbook — you want to stay and explore.
